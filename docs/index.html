    The transmitter in the corn

    For 31 years, WKRN-AM broadcast crop prices, tornado warnings and one very strange overnight jazz show to four counties. Then the license lapsed on a Tuesday.

[ The hosts ]

No headshots. Voiceprints.

You’ll never see their faces on this site — you’re here for their ears. Each portrait below is the host’s waveform, nothing else.

Mara Quist

Signal Path

Mixed live radio for twelve years. Owns three loudness meters, trusts none of them.

Errol Fane

Dead Air

Keeps a shelf of reel-to-reel tape rescued from station skips. Digitizes on Sundays.

Juno Reyes

The B-Side

Session bassist turned interviewer. Asks the question the liner notes never answered.

Sam Okafor

Night Bus

Records everything at 4 a.m. because, quote, “the city finally stops lying to the mic.”

Lena Vogl

Patch Notes

Built her first synth from a kit at fourteen. It still drifts sharp. She calls that a feature.
